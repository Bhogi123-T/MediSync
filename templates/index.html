How It Works

1

Patient Registration

Villagers register at a local rural health center or via ASHA workers on low-end smartphones. Profile is generated using available IDs.

2

Vitals & Symptoms Logging

Basic vitals and symptoms are entered in the local regional language. AI engine translates and processes the initial input.

3

AI Risk Assessment

The system evaluates the data for early markers of Diabetes, TB, or Hypertension, auto-flagging critical cases to priority queues.

4

Teleconsultation & e-Prescription

If required, a seamless low-bandwidth video call is initiated. Doctor provides diagnosis and an automated e-prescription is sent.

Impact & Viability

Aligned with UN SDGs 3 (Good Health), 10 (Reduced Inequalities), and 17 (Partnerships).

Advantages

  • 40% faster detection via AI early alerts.
  • Genuinely usable on 2G/3G via PWA architecture.
  • Free tier removes financial barriers for the poorest.
  • ABDM-aligned to plug into Ayushman Bharat schemes.
  • Eliminates travel for basic to intermediate consultations.
  • Auto-generated summaries for better follow-up care.

Challenges to Overcome

  • Digital literacy in rural areas makes self-onboarding hard.
  • ML accuracy heavily depends on scarce rural training data.
  • Supply constraint: Requires a large willing doctor workforce.
  • Regulatory compliance with NMC telemedicine guidelines.
  • Trust issues regarding digital health data storage.
  • Hardware access limitations for high-quality video calls.
